The ASCC Home Environment Sound Dataset

1. Introduction

This website introduces the ASCC Home Environment Sound Dataset. We totally collected 36 categories, 31,423 audios, 138,522 seconds. Some audios are collected from Google audioset [1], CHIME-HOME [2], SINS [3] and Eating Sound Collection [4], where the home environment-related audios are selected and processed. We also used web crawler technique to search and download realted audios uploaded by users from all over the wrold from Freesound [5]. Besides, we used a cell phone to record different audios and played them in our smart home environment[6]. The audios were played in different rooms and a laptop was used to record the audios played audio at different distance.

The original audios from different sources have different sample rates, durations and channels. We unified them into both 32kHz and 8kHz, 5 seconds, and mono. The sampling rate of 8kHz has less data, which is eaiser for a wearable device to process. For some sources, the durations of those audios are smaller than 5 seconds. Their duration is kept in the original shape. Those audios whose duration is larger than 5 seconds are cut into 5-second segments. After segmenting the audios, we manually verified the audios and removed the audios which have no sound event or unnoticeable sound event.
